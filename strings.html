The String Section

The strings section can be found at the front of the orchestra in a semi-circular arrangement, going from the highest pitch (violins) on the left, round to lower pitch (cellos and double basses) on the right.

These instruments are usually played using a bow made of wood and horse-hair to vibrate the strings and produce the distinctive sound. The strings are occassionally plucked using a technique known as "pizzicato".

The harp is not always used in every orchestral work, due to its invention many centuries after the rest of the section.
